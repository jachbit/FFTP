A robust futures trading plan is a written roadmap outlining your daily schedule, specific market focus, entry/exit criteria, and strict risk management rules. Designing a consistent strategy helps eliminate emotional decision-making.
1. Market Selection & Timeframes
Markets: Choose 1 to 2 highly liquid markets to specialize in — e.g. ES (E-mini S&P 500), NQ (E-mini Nasdaq 100), or CL (Crude Oil).
Timeframes: Identify whether you are a scalper, day trader, or swing trader. Most day traders use the 2-min, 5-min, or 15-min charts.
2. Daily Routine & Pre-Market Setup
Preparation: Log in 30–60 minutes before market open. Mark key levels from the overnight session: Previous Day Close, Previous Day High (PDH), Previous Day Low (PDL), and Overnight High/Low.
Trading Window: Stick to a designated active window — e.g. 8:30 AM – 11:00 AM ET — to avoid high-volatility, low-liquidity whipsaws.
3. Entry & Exit Criteria
Trigger: Define exactly what conditions must be met to enter a trade (e.g. price breaking and reclaiming a pre-market high, or a Volume Profile value area breakout). Do not take "gut" trades.
Profit Targets: Set precise targets using a fixed reward-to-risk ratio — e.g. 1.5:1 ($1.50 profit target for every $1 risked).
4. Risk & Leverage Management ⚠️
Stop-Loss (SL): Every trade must have a predefined SL to automatically cut losses if the setup fails.
Position Sizing: Never risk more than 1%–2% of your total account equity on a single trade. Be highly aware of initial margin and maintenance margin requirements.
First Loss Rule: After the first loss, cut your risk in half. If you risked 2% on the first trade, next trade is 1% only.
Stop Trading Rule: After two consecutive losses — STOP trading. Close the app and walk away. Do NOT revenge trade at all cost!!!
Trades Per Day: Discipline yourself to 1 to 2 max trades per day.
5. Post-Market Review
Trading Journal: Log every trade. Note the entry time, asset, setup type, profit/loss, and any emotional mistakes. Use the Trading Log tab in this app.
Continuous Adjustment: Review your journal weekly to refine your plan. Small improvements compound over time.
